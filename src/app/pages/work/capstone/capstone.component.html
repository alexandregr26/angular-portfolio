OVERVIEW

HealthChainON is a blockchain-powered proof of concept for a secure, interoperable, and patient-centric electronic health record system in Ontario. It was designed to explore how blockchain technology could address limitations in traditional Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems by improving transparency, strengthening patient control, and creating a more accountable way for health information to be stored, accessed, and shared.

THE PROBLEM

Ontario's health record systems remain fragmented across different healthcare providers, making it difficult for patients to access, control, and share their complete medical history. Records are often stored by individual institutions, which can lead to repeated data collection, delayed access, duplicated tests, and incomplete information during care. WHY BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY?

Blockchain is an electronic ledger where transactions are grouped into blocks and linked through cryptographic hashes, creating a secure and tamper-evident record of activity. Because health records contain highly sensitive personal information, they require a system that can verify access, preserve a reliable history of actions, and reduce dependence on any single institution to control patient data. Blockchain supports this by providing a transparent, immutable, and decentralized foundation for managing trusted record activity.
