Song Popularity Predictor

Project Description

With the exponential growth of music streaming services, breaking into the music industry has become increasingly challenging for new artists. As streaming platforms like Spotify rely heavily on algorithms that recommend songs based on their perceived popularity, it has become crucial for artists to create music that aligns with these algorithmic preferences. The objective of this project was to develop a predictive model that could forecast the popularity of a song based on its audio features. By leveraging deep learning techniques, specifically an Artificial Neural Network (ANN), this project aimed to provide artists with valuable insights into which songs are likely to gain the most exposure on streaming platforms, thereby improving their chances of success in a competitive industry.

Data Processing

The foundation of any predictive model lies in the quality and relevance of the data used for training. For this project, we utilized a combination of a pre-existing dataset from Kaggle and song features extracted directly from the Spotify API. The initial dataset comprised 174,000 songs, but our group applied several processing steps to refine this data to better suit our model's requirements.

The first step involved filtering out any songs released before the year 2000, as our focus was on current music trends. This reduced the dataset to 42,000 songs. We then eliminated any duplicate songs to prevent the model from being biased towards certain feature values. In cases where duplicates existed, the most popular version of the song was retained. This further refined the dataset to approximately 38,000 songs. To ensure the model could train effectively, we calculated the mean, median, and mode of the dataset’s popularity scores, aiming for a balanced distribution around a mean popularity rating of 50. Finally, we applied min-max normalization to all data values, standardizing them between 0 and 1. This normalization was crucial for speeding up the model's learning process and handling the variability between different feature values. Additionally, we implemented a weighted cross-entropy function to address class imbalance, ensuring the model did not favor any particular class.

ANN Model

The heart of this project was the development and training of a deep learning model using an Artificial Neural Network (ANN). The choice of an ANN was driven by its ability to learn complex, non-linear relationships between input features and the target output—in this case, the song's popularity.

The architecture of our ANN consisted of four fully connected layers. The first three layers were composed of 1,000, 2,000, and 4,000 hidden units, respectively, and utilized the ReLU activation function. This setup allowed the model to capture intricate patterns in the data by assigning weights to different song features. The output layer consisted of five neurons, each corresponding to a specific popularity class ranging from 0 to 4. These classes were designed to simplify the prediction process and reduce variance, making it easier for the model to achieve accurate results. Our model was trained over multiple epochs, and we carefully tuned hyperparameters to achieve the best possible performance.

Results

The ANN model we developed demonstrated significant improvements over the baseline model - a decision tree classifier. While the decision tree achieved a baseline accuracy of 45%, our ANN model outperformed it with a testing accuracy of 55%. To further evaluate the model's performance, we also examined its accuracy across different popularity classes. The model excelled in predicting songs in the mid-range popularity classes (2 and 3), achieving accuracies of 53.4% and 58.8%, respectively. However, it struggled with songs at the extremities of the popularity spectrum, particularly in classes 1 and 4, where accuracy dropped significantly. Despite these challenges, the model provided valuable insights into the relationship between a song's audio features and its potential success on streaming platforms.

Overall, this project showcased the potential of applying deep learning to predict song popularity. As my first AI-related project, it was both challenging and rewarding, resulting in a model that outperformed traditional methods and provided valuable insights. This experience has deepened my interest in AI and its practical applications in the music industry and beyond.
