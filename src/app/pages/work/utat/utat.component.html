Project Description

In 2020, as a member of the University of Toronto Aerospace Team (UTAT), I was assigned to design a low-power sensor for the Ground Station of the Defiance rocket project. The Defiance rocket, a hybrid rocket that had been under development since 2017, was a rocket designed by UTAT that aimed to surpass the Canadian amateur altitude record at 13.4 km. The Ground Station was a crucial part of the Defiance project, orchestrating the rocket's tracking and managing critical test and fill procedures on its launch pad. The low-power sensor, an integral part of the Ground Station, was strategically designed to address potential emergencies that might lead to the rocket's launch abortion. Unfortunately, the complete testing of this sensor design faced barriers due to the COVID-19 pandemic, as essential components were located on campus and strict access restrictions were in place.

Requirements

  • Automatically turn off the circuit when the 22V battery reaches 18V to protect the circuit's components.
  • Have a minimum amount of current flowing in the circuit once it is turned off.

Components

  • 22.2 Lithium-Ion Battery
  • LT1495 Op Amp
  • LT1389 Zener Diode (Voltage Reference)
  • MOSFET Transistor
