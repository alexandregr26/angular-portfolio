Project Description

In 2020, as a member of the University of Toronto Aerospace Team (UTAT), I designed a low-power sensor for the Ground Station of the Defiance Rocket Project - a rocket developed by UTAT that aimed to surpass the Canadian amateur altitude record at 13.4 km. The Ground Station was a crucial part of the Defiance rocket that managed critical test and fill procedures at the launch pad. One of its integral components was this low-power sensor, which was intended to address potential emergencies that might lead to the rocket's launch abortion. Unfortunately, this sensor design was never fully tested or integrated into the Defiance rocket since its development came to a complete standstill due to the COVID-19 pandemic, which imposed strict access restrictions on essential components stored at the UofT campus.

Requirements

  • Automatically turn off the circuit when the 22V battery reaches 18V to protect the circuit's components.
  • Have a minimum amount of current flowing in the circuit once it is turned off.

Components

  • 22.2 Lithium-Ion Battery
  • LT1495 Op Amp
  • LT1389 Zener Diode (Voltage Reference)
  • MOSFET Transistor
