About Me

Technology shapes how people think, work, and interact with the world. I care about building systems that do that thoughtfully.

I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, with a minor in Business and Artificial Intelligence. Since then, my work has moved through product, platform, and infrastructure — from financial technology and operational systems at Coinbase, to developer tooling at Thomson Reuters, to customer-facing systems at Amazon.

What interests me is not software in the abstract. It is software as part of something larger: the systems people use to work, decide, trust, and move through complexity. Software is never just software. It lives inside the world, and at its best, it makes that world clearer, more reliable, and easier to act within. To me, good engineering is not only about performance or scale, but about judgment, elegance, and the ability to create technology people can genuinely trust.

That is the standard I care about. I want to build systems that matter — systems whose value is felt beyond the codebase. I am not interested in technology as decoration. I want my work to hold its weight in the world outside it.
