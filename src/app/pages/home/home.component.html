About Me

I'm an engineer interested in building systems that move the world forward — not just software that ships, but technology that creates clarity, accelerates decisions, and improves lives.

My background sits across engineering, business, and artificial intelligence. I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, and have worked at Amazon, Thomson Reuters, and Coinbase across product engineering, developer platforms, production infrastructure, and AI-powered operational tooling.

Across these experiences, I've become drawn to complex environments where technology, people, and decision-making collide. Markets, history, policy, culture, and human behavior all shape how systems are built, adopted, and experienced — because technology does not exist in isolation. It lives inside the world, and it should improve the world it touches.

What I want to build is simple to say and hard to do: systems that matter. Tools that clarify instead of confuse, accelerate without dehumanizing, strengthen human judgment, and improve the lives of the people they serve.

I’m looking for ambitious teams operating at the edge of what is possible — teams building with real stakes, real users, and the potential to leave the world better than they found it.
