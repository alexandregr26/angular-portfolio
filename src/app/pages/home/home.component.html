About Me

Technology shapes how people think, work, and interact with the world. I care about building systems that do that thoughtfully.

My background sits across engineering, business, and artificial intelligence. I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto and have worked at Amazon, Thomson Reuters, and Coinbase, where I’ve been exposed to production infrastructure, product engineering, financial technology, and user-facing software.

What ties those experiences together is the kind of engineer I want to become: someone who can build deeply, think clearly, and communicate across technical and human contexts. I care about systems that are reliable under pressure, products that feel intentional, and technology worthy of the people who depend on them.

The work I want to do is simple to say and difficult to execute: build systems that matter. To me, good engineering is not only about performance or scale, but also judgment, elegance, and the ability to create technology people genuinely trust and enjoy using. That is the standard I build toward.
